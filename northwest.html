Northwest Region

The Northwest region is known for its rich culinary traditions and vibrant flavors. Popular dishes include "Achu" and "Yellow Soup." The Northwest region of Cameroon boasts a rich culinary heritage characterized by vibrant flavors and traditional dishes that reflect the area's cultural diversity. "Achu" and "Yellow Soup" are two popular dishes from this region that symbolize communal gatherings and celebrations. Achu, a starchy dish made from grated cocoyams and seasoned with palm oil, peanuts, and spices, is traditionally prepared during family feasts and important cultural events. The use of bitterleaf, a key ingredient in Achu, gives it a unique flavor that represents the traditional tastes of the Northwest. "Yellow Soup," often made with meat or fish, serves as a delicious and hearty stew that's enjoyed during special occasions, weddings, and festive celebrations. Both dishes highlight the Northwest's culinary emphasis on combining fresh local ingredients with rich spices and flavors, and they showcase the region's tradition of communal eating and social interaction.
