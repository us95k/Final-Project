Southwest Region

The Southwest region offers a variety of seafood-based dishes like "Fufu and Eru" " showcasing the influence of coastal traditions. The Southwest region of Cameroon is renowned for its rich culinary heritage, characterized by its bold and diverse flavors that reflect both the local culture and the region's natural abundance. One of the most iconic dishes from this region is "Fufu and Eru," a beloved meal that symbolizes the unity and togetherness of Cameroonian families. "Fufu" is a starchy, smooth dough-like dish made from fermented cassava or cocoyam, which serves as the perfect accompaniment to "Eru," a flavorful stew made from the leaves of the Eru plant (also known as wild spinach) and often cooked with meat, fish, and spices. The dish is deeply rooted in tradition, often enjoyed during communal meals and family gatherings.
