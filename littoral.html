Littoral Region

The Littoral region of Cameroon is celebrated for its vibrant and diverse culinary traditions, heavily influenced by its coastal location and access to fresh seafood, as well as its blend of indigenous and colonial cultural influences. One of the most iconic dishes from this region is Ndole, a flavorful and aromatic stew made primarily from bitterleaf, peanuts, and a variety of meats or fish. This dish holds significant cultural value, often served during important communal gatherings, celebrations, and family meals. The preparation of Ndole involves a delicate balance of flavors, with the bitterness of the leaves complementing the richness of the peanut paste, and the meat or fish providing a savory depth. It is typically enjoyed with plantains, cassava, or fufu, making it a hearty and satisfying meal.
